# Bank Term Deposit Subscription Analysis (Python)

## Project Overview
This project analyzes bank marketing campaign data to identify **key factors influencing customer subscription to term deposits**.  
Using Python-based data analysis, the study uncovers **customer behavior patterns**, campaign effectiveness, and actionable insights to improve subscription conversion rates.

The objective is to support **data-driven decision-making** for targeted marketing and customer engagement strategies in the banking domain.

---

## Business Problem
Banks invest heavily in marketing campaigns, yet conversion rates for term deposit subscriptions remain low.  
The challenge is to understand:
- Which customers are more likely to subscribe
- Which campaign attributes drive success
- How banks can optimize outreach strategies

---

## Dataset Description
The dataset contains customer-level banking information including:
- Demographics (age, job, marital status, education)
- Financial attributes (balance, loans)
- Campaign interaction details (contact type, call duration, previous outcomes)
- Target variable: **Term Deposit Subscription (Yes/No)**

## Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
Key analyses performed:

- Campaign contact behavior analysis

---

## Key Insights (Results)
- **Call duration is the strongest predictor** of term deposit subscription  
- Customers with **positive outcomes in previous campaigns** show significantly higher conversion rates  
- Certain job categories demonstrate higher responsiveness to term deposit offers  
- Customers with **higher account balances** are more likely to subscribe  
- Repeated contact without prior success reduces conversion probability  

---

## Business Recommendations
- Prioritize customers with successful past campaign interactions  
- Optimize call duration strategy to improve engagement quality  
- Focus marketing efforts on high-response customer segments  
- Reduce repeated outreach to low-probability customers to lower campaign costs
